Thank You


Thank you for participating in MindTrails. Your involvement is very important to the growth of the program. Ultimately, our goal is to create an accessible and cost-effective program that reduces symptoms of anxiety, and we cannot reach that goal without you.

You did a lot as a MindTrails participant. During your weekly training sessions, you read about ambiguous emotional situations that were designed to help you develop more flexible, less rigidly negative thinking patterns that you could use in your everyday life.

MindTrails uses cognitive bias modification for interpretation (CBM-I) to help you develop more flexible thinking skills. It does this by targeting cognitive biases, which are patterns of thinking that relate to our emotional responses, and affect how we think about situations. People with anxiety often assume a negative outcome. Through repeated practice, CBM targets your cognitive biases to teach you how to interpret information in new ways so you can consider different perspectives. This is why we had you complete five training sessions over the course of five weeks!

If you are interested in learning more about the science behind MindTrails, check out these papers that were written by members of the MindTrails team and include data from the MindTrails study: The Science of MindTrails

We'd love to hear feedback from you on your experience with the program. You can provide feedback now by emailing us at studyteam@mindtrails.org.

Lastly, don't forget to complete the six-month follow-up survey. Your responses to this survey will give us a better understanding of what MindTrails was like for you and how we can improve the program for future participants. Also, it will help us understand how people are managing their anxiety during this particularly challenging time with the coronavirus. We will remind you about the survey by email in the coming weeks.

Thank you again for participating in MindTrails.
