Feature Articles: Horror Movies May Help You Feel Less Afraid

This may seem like it doesn't make sense, but horror movies may actually help you become less afraid of what you fear most.

Horror movies might operate as a form of exposure therapy. Kristen Lindgren, Ph.D., talks about this in the Right as Rain by UW Medicine article "Is Watching Horror Movies Bad for Your Mental Health?" written by Mckenna Princing. Exposure therapy engages clients with something that scares them, such as spiders or heights. Over time, the client learns how to handle their fear when they are faced with the thing that scares them. Lindgren explains that horror movies can act as a type of exposure therapy because they expose viewers to the very things that they're afraid of (Princing, 2018).

So, if you're thinking about watching a horror movie, consider choosing one that's about something you're afraid of. It could help you learn how to handle your fear. However, don't feel bad if you don't think that's the right choice for you. They're called horror movies for a reason!
